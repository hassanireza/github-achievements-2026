// 05 - Read this before you "hack" anything

The sock-puppet account trick isn't a hack. It's a self-report.

You'll see guides telling you to spin up a second account to co-author commits with yourself for Pair Extraordinaire. Here's why that's the worst trade in this whole guide.

⚠️

GitHub's Terms of Service cap you at one free personal account.

Section 3 of the GitHub Terms of Service states that one person or legal entity may maintain no more than one free Account. Creating a second account purely to co-author commits with yourself is exactly the "manipulate platform features" pattern GitHub's trust & safety team looks for - and enforcement has gotten sharper, not softer, every year. The badge is cosmetic. The suspension risk is to your real account, your real contribution history, and any orgs you belong to.

✕ The sock-puppet method

  • Violates GitHub ToS §3 (one free account per person)
  • Trivially detectable: shared IP, device fingerprint, timing patterns, identical commit cadence
  • Risk is asymmetric - you risk your real account to dress up a fake one
  • Org admins and security teams flag linked-account patterns automatically
  • Saves you maybe 10 minutes versus the legitimate path below

✓ The legitimate method

  • Fully ToS-compliant - uses Git's real co-authoring feature as designed
  • Pair with literally anyone: a friend, a study-group peer, a Discord server buddy, a coworker
  • Builds an actual collaboration habit and commit etiquette that helps your career
  • Works for the entire Pair Extraordinaire tier ladder (10 → 24 → 48 PRs), not just the base badge
  • Zero account risk, same badge, same shelf life on your profile
